# Hyperbolic Gravity Chamber — Deep Research Report (verified)

Source for `campaigns/ARC_GRAVITY`. Multi-source deep research, 105 agents,
adversarially verified (1 refuted). Raw: `gravity-research-raw.json`.

## The honesty finding (shaped the whole campaign framing)

**Slow tempo does NOT build more muscle or strength than normal tempo.**
Three converging 2025-2026 peer-reviewed meta-analyses: eccentric-phase
duration effects on strength (g=0.18) and hypertrophy (g=0.03) are "unclear";
the "How slow should you go?" review of 14 studies found similar hypertrophy
for faster (0.43) vs slower (0.34) tempos, trivially favoring FASTER. So
"slower = more gains" is marketing, not science. (One hard limit: reps over
~10s/rep ARE inferior — the campaign caps every rep under 10s.)

→ The campaign is framed as a SAFE, CONTROLLED tension / strength-endurance /
focus / discipline method, NOT as superior muscle growth. The "gravity
multiplier" and slow-tempo theming are motivational packaging, flagged as
fantasy, never a real training variable.

## What IS real (used)

- **Eccentric-emphasis is a genuine STRENGTH stimulus** (pooled ES ~0.95;
  moderate hypertrophy ~0.60, mostly clinical/older cohorts). REFUTED (0-3,
  not claimed): that eccentric is *superior* to concentric.
- **Isometric and tempo work are legitimate trainable methods** for beginners.

## Safety — the backbone (all peer-reviewed, encoded as hard rules)

- **Breathe, never Valsalva.** Breath-holding during isometrics/slow grinding
  reps sharply amplifies the BP surge — a full Valsalva max lift hit mean
  311/284 (peak 370/360) mmHg vs 198/175 with open-glottis exhalation; even a
  breath-hold *without* full Valsalva significantly raised MAP and cerebral
  artery velocity. → core rule + a breath-hold stop flag on every module.
  (Absolute pressures are far lower in light bodyweight work, but the
  directional rule holds regardless.)
- **Novel eccentric/high-TUT reliably causes DOMS**; the first unaccustomed
  bout does the most damage. → gradual gravity-stage ramp.
- **Repeated-bout effect**: repeating submaximal eccentric sessions confers
  protection (less DOMS, lower CK/LDH). → validates gradual staging and the
  deliberate Stage 9 back-off before the finale.
- **Exertional rhabdomyolysis is a real risk even after LOW-intensity work**,
  with unaccustomed exercise a recognized precipitant — the beginner-ramping-
  too-fast profile. → capped novel volume, target-based finale, and stop
  criteria (severe/unusual muscle pain, swelling, dark urine).
- **Orthostatic caution**: heavy fatiguing isometrics can impair standing BP
  regulation (dizziness on standing) — reinforces light-load + short-hold
  guidance.
- **Target hold times, never "unlimited"/to-failure** — the sources establish
  to-failure is risky; replacement targets are set by conservative convention
  (plank 20/40/60s, wall-sit 20/30/45s, horse-stance 20/30/45s, squat-hold
  10/20/30s by Easy/Normal/Hard).
