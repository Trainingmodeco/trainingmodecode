# Garou (Hero Hunter) — Deep Research Report (verified)

Source for `campaigns/ARC_GAROU`. Multi-source deep research, 107 agents,
adversarially verified (21 confirmed / 4 refuted). Raw: `garou-research-raw.json`.

Scope: evidence-based methods for (A) a lean, explosive fighter-sprinter
physique and (B) fast, explosive striking (boxing / Dutch kickboxing / Wing
Chun), for a general/beginner user training mostly solo at home — plus how to
progress a dual-path (FIT / FIGHT / BOTH), 10-stage campaign safely.

## The honesty findings (shaped the campaign framing)

- **"One-punch power" is fantasy.** Plyometric/ballistic training reliably
  improves punch SPEED, rate of force development, and power-ENDURANCE, but does
  NOT reliably raise raw single-shot maximum punch force in the short term. (A
  dramatic "8-week plyo = ~25% punch-force gain" claim was REFUTED 0-3.) So the
  campaign trains fast, explosive, well-conditioned striking — never a max
  "knockout" stat. Mid-fight "evolving" and monster transformation are pure
  fiction; the real evolution is skill + speed + conditioning over time.
- **Spot reduction is a myth.** A 2021 meta-analysis (13 studies, ~1,158 people;
  pooled ES -0.03, p=0.51) and a 12-week RCT confirm localized training does not
  reduce localized fat. Ab work builds the muscle but does NOT reveal it. Visible
  abs come from whole-body fat loss.
- **Leanness is a nutrition/energy-balance outcome.** Exercise-alone weight loss
  averages only ~2-3 kg. Moderate aerobic exercise is Grade A for fat loss;
  moderate-to-high resistance training is Grade A for sparing the muscle that
  makes abs/arms/legs defined in a deficit. You cannot out-train a poor diet.

## What IS real (verified, used)

- **Explosive lower-body work builds fast hands.** Plyometrics and jump rope
  (8 wk, 3×/wk) improved CMJ rate of force development, punch reaction time,
  rear-leg ground reaction force, and jab velocity — jump rope ≈ formal
  plyometrics, making it a viable minimal-equipment substitute. (Subjects were
  trained junior boxers, small n — directional for beginners.)
- **Lower-body strength — not upper-body — drives punch force.** In trained
  amateur boxers, CMJ and isometric mid-thigh-pull peak forces correlated
  moderately-to-strongly with peak punch force; no upper-body measure did. →
  program explosive legs (jumps, squats, sprints) as the punch-power driver.
  (State the positive: "legs drive the punch"; the strong negative about
  upper-body was only weakly supported.)
- **Med-ball throws / upper-body plyometrics add ballistic power** (a striking
  proxy): 2023 meta-analysis (35 studies, 1,412 participants) — medicine-ball
  throw ES 0.64 (moderate), sport-specific throwing ES 0.55 (small). Certainty
  low/very-low; include as home-friendly power work.
- **Contrast/complex (variable-resistance) training improves punch speed, force,
  and power** (6-wk elite study, ES 1.17-1.79) — but small/elite sample, no true
  control; position as an ADVANCED method (Stage 8), not beginner.
- **Safe plyo dosing:** ~50-200 foot-contacts/session (beginner ~60-100,
  intermediate ~80-120, advanced up to ~120-198), 48h between moderate sessions
  / 72h between high-intensity, no back-to-back plyo days. Medium confidence
  (PT-blog echoing NSCA/NASM consensus + a 2025 RCT on 2× vs 3×/wk).

## REFUTED (not used as literal content)

- "8-week plyometrics → ~25% straight-punch force gain" (0-3).
- A trunk-fat RCT claimed as proof spot reduction exists (1-2).
- The strong claim that upper-body strength is irrelevant to punch force (1-2) —
  so we use the positive framing (legs drive the punch), not the strong negative.

## Fantasy (flavor only, never trainable content)

Superhuman speed/strength, "one-punch" knockout power, mid-fight "evolving," and
monster transformation. The Hero-Hunter / martial-evolution theming is
motivational packaging.

## NOT established by the verified research (conventional coaching, flagged)

The striking-TECHNIQUE pillars themselves — boxing/Dutch-kickboxing/Wing-Chun
fundamentals, chain punching, trapping, centerline theory, reaction/reflex
drills, shoulder/rotator-cuff injury-resilience protocols, and hand/wrist
protection for bag work — did NOT surface as verified claims. They are built
from standard, safe coaching convention and labeled as such in the data (esp.
Wing Chun, where evidence for specific traditional claims is limited — trained
here for speed and structure, not as proven combat efficacy).

## Caveats / integrity note

Nearly all striking studies used trained/elite/junior boxers (small n, some
female-only, several without a non-exercise control), so transfer to home
beginners is plausible but not directly tested. Safety numbers rest on
certification-consensus (medium confidence). Two sources (boxingfitness.com,
performancepurpose.ca) were SECURITY-FLAGGED during the run for probing the
agent proxy status endpoint and were EXCLUDED from synthesis.

## Open questions (carried forward)

- Evidence base for the specific striking-technique / traditional-martial-art
  pillars (boxing/Dutch/Wing Chun efficacy, trapping, centerline).
- Evidence-based shoulder/rotator-cuff resilience and hand/wrist protection
  protocols for strikers.
- Whether reaction/reflex drills improve beginner striking reaction time beyond
  general plyometric gains.
- Validated starting volumes/intensities for true home beginners (vs.
  extrapolation from trained-athlete studies).
