# Baki Campaign — Deep Research Report (verified)

Source for `campaigns/ARC_BAKI`. Multi-source deep research, 68 agents,
claims adversarially verified (0 refuted; several fantasy elements FLAGGED
as non-trainable). Raw output: `baki-research-raw.json`.

## Confirmed findings used in the campaign

**Fan/creator program structure** (SuperheroJacked, high conf.)
- Calisthenics-primary: Upper / Lower / Full-Body strength split + 3 HIIT
  days + dedicated ab/core sessions + added MMA work, on progressive
  overload with high-rep/high-speed pull-ups and push-ups → the campaign's
  fit spine (calisthenics base, HIIT, iron core).
- **Shadow-boxing IS the real "phantom" training** — the trainable stand-in
  for Baki's imaginary-opponent fighting; paired with jab technique and
  explosiveness → the "phantom rounds" motif (13 across the campaign) +ThePhantom achievement.
- SuperheroJacked maintains a **per-character library** (Katsumi karate,
  Retsu kung fu, etc.) → validates the per-stage per-fighter mapping.

**Canon character themes** (Baki wiki, adequate for fiction)
- **Yujiro "the Ogre"**: ~6'3"/265 lb strength/size benchmark; favors
  simple fundamentals + strength over borrowed technique; power is partly
  MENTAL ("mental capacity far stronger than any muscle") → boss stage as an
  all-around + composure/mental-dominance test.
- **Jack**: unrefined brute-force brawler → raw size/power stage (themed).

## FLAGGED as anime fantasy — lore/imagery ONLY, never training content

- **Jack**: "super-steroids," "30 hours a day" training, surgically
  implanted titanium-alloy teeth ("Biting Path"), bare-handed polar-bear
  kill, punching a helicopter. → The Jack stage is normal, safe heavy
  strength work with an explicit fantasy disclaimer. NO PEDs, NO extreme
  bulk, NO 30-hr training reproduced.
- **Yujiro**: Demon Back / Demon Face is a genetic bloodline trait, "not so
  much a technique as a predetermined advantage" → shown as lore with a
  "⚠ anime fantasy — not a training goal" tag; the real lesson (composure,
  fundamentals) is what the boss trains.
- All superhuman feats → imagery only.

## Not well-sourced (handled conservatively, flagged as open questions)

The research did NOT surface primary sourcing on: makiwara/knuckle-
conditioning injury risk, grip-strength protocols, kung-fu stance work
specifics, or overtraining limits. The campaign therefore:
- Replaces bare-knuckle makiwara board conditioning with **safe heavy-bag
  work + wrist/knuckle alignment + gradual volume**, and flags makiwara as
  traditional-but-injury-risky (arthritis/joint risk). Stops on hand pain.
- Uses well-established grip work (dead hangs, pinch, farmer holds,
  thick/towel grip) for the Hanayama stage.
- Caps volume and uses predetermined targets on the peak/HIIT stages (no
  max-until-collapse), consistent with the Berserk Eclipse safety approach.

## Design inferences (not from sources)

Persona→stage mapping and the 12-stage arc (Young Baki → Underground →
Katsumi → Retsu → Hanayama → Izou → Jack → Pickle → peak → Yujiro boss) are
the campaign's own design, grounded in the confirmed per-character themes.
The core rule «Chase the monster, train like a human.» encodes the
fantasy-vs-real safety principle directly.
