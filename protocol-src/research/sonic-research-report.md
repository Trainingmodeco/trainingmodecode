# Sonic (Speed Protocol) — Deep Research Report (verified)

Source for `campaigns/ARC_SONIC`. Multi-source deep research, 105 agents,
adversarially verified (24 confirmed / 1 refuted). Raw: `sonic-research-raw.json`.

Scope: evidence-based methods to build running speed, sprint acceleration,
agility, and explosive power for a general/beginner user training mostly at
home/solo with minimal equipment — and how to progress a 12-stage speed campaign
SAFELY. FIT-ONLY, no fight pillar.

## The headline

A safe beginner speed program converges on four things: (1) build explosiveness
with progressive plyometrics, capped and counted; (2) develop top speed with
low-volume, high-quality max-velocity sprints and full recovery; (3) ALWAYS
precede high-intensity work with a mandatory structured neuromuscular/dynamic
warm-up; (4) manage load progression and condition the vulnerable links
(hamstrings, ankles). All of the content below is real, trainable method. The
only cartoon-fantasy elements — superhuman top speed and flips/tricks — are
flavor only and never become training content.

## What IS real (verified, used)

- **Mandatory neuromuscular warm-up (high confidence).** A ~15-min FIFA 11+ style
  warm-up (joint mobility, activation, dynamic stretch, progressive intensity)
  BOTH reduces lower-extremity injury AND acutely improves sprint, jump, and
  balance. Backed by a 2026 PRISMA systematic review of 40 studies and a n=180
  parkour survey (skipped warm-ups → significantly higher injury counts). → the
  campaign's WARM-UP GATE mechanic.
- **Plyometric foot-contact caps (high).** Beginner ~60-80 foot-contacts per
  session (NSCA CSCS Ch.18 tiers; conservative floor 60), scaling with
  experience (int. ~100-120, adv. ~120-140; 50-200 envelope). Sets of 5-10 reps,
  ≥1 min rest. → Easy/Normal/Hard caps 40/60/80 (Hard sits at the TOP of the
  beginner band, never above).
- **Plyo recovery + progression (high).** ≥48h (2-3 days) between plyometric
  sessions; low-amplitude pogo/ankle/line hops can tolerate ~24h. Progress by
  intensity (skipping/squat jumps → lateral/tuck jumps → box/depth jumps).
  Bilateral before unilateral. On pain: cut foot-contacts 50%, land soft with
  bent knees (never stiff).
- **Max-velocity sprints: low-volume/high-quality (medium).** 40-60m sprints
  (last 10-30m at top speed), 3-4 reps, ~120-240m per session, FULL recovery
  between reps. Single reputable coaching source (SimpliFaster) → medium
  confidence; corroborated by broad practitioner consensus.
- **Eccentric hamstring work (high).** Nordic curls raise eccentric strength
  (~10-26%) and fascicle length and cut hamstring injuries (up to ~51% with high
  adherence). Part of a combined package (warm-up + eccentric strength + dynamic
  stretch + progressive load) that lowers injury and supports performance.
- **Both load spikes AND lack of conditioning independently raise injury risk
  (high).** So a safe program combines managed volume progression with dedicated
  conditioning. Experienced practitioners themselves supplement skill work
  (~76% stretch, ~75.6% do physical conditioning).
- **Ankle is the highest-risk parkour joint (high).** 2.3/1000h vs knee 1.1/1000h
  (overall ~1.7/1000h, mostly ankle sprains). → ankle stability + landing
  mechanics are the top priority in beginner scaffolding (Stage 2 comes before
  any jumps).
- **Land/roll before advanced moves (high).** Beginners must master safe landing
  (absorb with bent knees, never "stick") and the safety roll BEFORE any jumps or
  tricks. Foundational beginner set: Landing, Two-Handed Vault, Wall Climb-Up
  (muscle-up), Precision Jump. Exclude flips.
- **Recovery = fundamentals (high).** Sleep, nutrition, hydration — not gadgets;
  recovery-modality evidence is heterogeneous.

## REFUTED (not used as literal content)

- **"~100 landings before each height increment" (1-2, refuted).** The
  underlying principle — progress landing height gradually, master landings
  before adding height — is sound and IS used. The specific rep-count-per-height
  scheme is not evidence-based; copy says "master before progressing," never a
  hard number.

## Fantasy (flavor only, never trainable content)

- Superhuman top speed; flips and tricks. Sonic "speed multiplier" / zone theming
  is motivational packaging only.

## Not established by sources (conservative convention, flagged)

The verified research did NOT prescribe: exact per-stage rep/distance/rest
numbers; agility-drill parameters (ladder/5-10-5/T-drill sets/reps/rest); a
concrete beginner running-volume ramp rate (the ~10%/week heuristic used here is
convention, not from a verified source); acceleration-phase/hill-sprint dosing;
or a beginner deload cadence (Stage 11). These are built from standard
conservative programming practice and labeled as such in the data.

## Sourcing / integrity note

Strong primary sources anchor the warm-up/injury-prevention and parkour-
epidemiology findings (2026 PRISMA review of 40 studies; 2022 peer-reviewed
survey of n=180). Plyometric volume/recovery/progression numbers come from
trainer-cert texts and PT-clinic blogs faithfully relaying NSCA CSCS Ch.18
guidance (independently corroborated). The sprint structure rests on a single
reputable coaching publication (medium confidence). One source
(garagegymreviews.com) was SECURITY-FLAGGED during the run for probing the agent
proxy status endpoint ($HTTPS_PROXY/__agentproxy/status), unrelated to its task —
it was EXCLUDED from synthesis.
